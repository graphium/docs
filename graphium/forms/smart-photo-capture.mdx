## Overview

Smart Photo Capture lets you digitize paper forms by simply photographing them with your phone or camera. Graphium AI analyzes the image, identifies each field, and extracts the data automatically — no manual typing required. This is one of Graphium's most powerful features for high-volume paper form digitization.

## When to Use This

Use Smart Photo Capture whenever your team receives physical paper forms that need to be entered into the system — such as patient intake forms, field inspection sheets, delivery receipts, or paper surveys.

## Prerequisites

- You are signed in to Graphium AI (mobile browser or app)
- The relevant Vault and Photo Form have already been set up by an administrator (see [Designing Forms](/graphium/forms/designing-forms))
- You have a mobile device with a working camera, or access to a flatbed scanner

## Tips for a Great Photo

The quality of your photo directly affects extraction accuracy. Before capturing:

- **Lay the form flat** on a clean, solid-color surface
- **Use good lighting** — natural daylight or a well-lit desk works best; avoid shadows across the form
- **Avoid glare** from overhead lights or windows
- **Frame the form fully** — all four corners of the document should be visible in the viewfinder
- **Hold the camera still** — blurry images lead to extraction errors

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Open the Vault and Select the Form

In Graphium AI on your mobile device, navigate to the relevant **Vault**, then tap the **Forms** tab. Select the **Photo Form** that matches the paper form you're holding.

### Step 2: Tap "Capture Photo"

Tap the **Capture Photo** button to open the camera interface (or **Upload Photo** if you have a scan already saved to your device).

### Step 3: Position and Photograph the Form

Frame the paper form within the camera view using the on-screen guides. Once it's centered and in focus, tap to capture the photo. Graphium will display a preview — if the image is blurry, shadowed, or cut off, retake it before proceeding.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the form has multiple pages, capture each page as a separate submission or use the multi-page option if available in your form settings.

### Step 4: Review AI-Extracted Data

After capturing, Graphium AI processes the image and displays the extracted field values alongside the original photo. Review each field carefully:

- Values shown in **green** were extracted with high confidence
- Values shown in **yellow** were uncertain and need your review
- Values shown in **red** were not extracted and require manual entry

### Step 5: Correct Any Errors

Tap any field to edit its value. Correct any misread characters, dates, or numbers. Pay special attention to handwritten sections, which are more prone to variation.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the same field is consistently misread across multiple submissions, ask your administrator to re-annotate the bounding box in the Photo Form Designer with a tighter area.

### Step 6: Submit

Once all fields are verified, tap **Submit**. The record is saved to the Vault and becomes immediately available in the **Table View** for your team.

## What to Expect

Submitted records appear in the Vault's Table View within seconds of submission. If a **Workflow** is connected to the Vault, it may trigger automatically — for example, routing the record for a supervisor's review or exporting it to an external system via a **Connection**.

## Common Issues & Troubleshooting

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Most fields are red (not extracted) | Photo is blurry, dark, or misaligned | Retake the photo in better lighting and hold the camera steady |
| Handwritten values are consistently wrong | Handwriting is very stylized | Manually correct and submit; report to your admin to improve the model |
| Form not available in the mobile view | Form is inactive | Ask your administrator to activate the form in the Forms Manager |
| Photo upload button is missing | Wrong form type selected | Ensure you've selected the Photo Form, not the Keyboard form |
| Submission fails | Poor network connection | Check your internet connection and try submitting again; data is saved locally if offline and will sync when reconnected |
