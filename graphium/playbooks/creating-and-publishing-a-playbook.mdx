## Overview

Builders can create a playbook from the Playbooks section of any vault they have access to. Setting up a playbook involves giving it a name, writing its steps, choosing a trigger, granting access to the vault resources it needs, and publishing a version to make it active.

This article walks through each part of that process.

---

## When to Create a Playbook

Create a playbook when you need to:

- Automate a recurring workflow that currently requires manual steps
- Run a multi-step AI process against your vault data on a schedule
- Standardize a process so it runs consistently every time without human coordination

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Open the Playbooks Section

From within your vault, select **Playbooks** in the left sidebar. If you do not see this option, confirm your vault role is **ADMIN** or **BUILDER** — Contributors and Viewers do not have access to Playbooks.

Click **+ New Playbook** in the top-right corner of the Playbooks page.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/graphium/playbooks/new-playbook-button.png" alt="New Playbook button" />
</Frame>

---

### Step 2: Name Your Playbook

A **Create Playbook** modal appears. Enter a name for your playbook — for example, *Nightly Provider Performance Report* or *Weekly Missing Documentation Check* — and click **+ Create Playbook**.

The playbook editor opens immediately.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/graphium/playbooks/create-playbook-modal.png" alt="Create Playbook modal" />
</Frame>

---

### Step 3: Understand the Editor Layout

The playbook editor has three columns:

- **Left — Playbook Outline:** Contains the Playbook Context card, the trigger, and the list of steps. The **Save & Test** button is also here.
- **Center — Step Details:** Shows the configuration for whichever step or section is selected in the outline.
- **Right — Run History:** Shows all past and in-progress runs for this playbook.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/graphium/playbooks/playbook-editor-overview.png" alt="Playbook editor three-column layout" />
</Frame>

---

### Step 4: Write the Steps

In the **Playbook Outline**, your playbook starts with a default **Step 1**. Click it to open its configuration in the **Step Details** panel.

Each step has three fields:

- **Step Title** — A short label that identifies the step in the outline (for example, *Query Today's Case Data*).
- **Summary** — A brief description of what this step does. This can be written manually or generated by the AI using the **AI-generated** option.
- **Instructions** — The detailed, plain-language instructions that tell the AI exactly what to do during this step. The Instructions field supports rich text formatting.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/graphium/playbooks/step-details.png" alt="Step details panel showing Step Title, Summary, and Instructions fields" />
</Frame>

> 💡 **Tip:** Write instructions in plain language and be specific. Reference exact table names and field values where helpful — for example: *"From the Cases table, select all rows where case_date = today and status is Completed or Signed."*

To manage steps in the outline:

- **Add a step** — Click **+** in the Steps section header to add a new step below the last one.
- **Reorder steps** — Drag a step using its drag handle to change its order.
- **Remove a step** — Click the delete icon on the step you want to remove.

> **Note:** Steps run in sequence. The output of one step is passed to the next, so you can chain logic across steps.

---

### Step 5: Configure the Trigger

Click the **trigger card** in the Playbook Outline (below Playbook Context) to open Trigger Configuration in the center panel.

Select a trigger type:

- **Manual** — The playbook only runs when a Builder clicks **Save & Test** or triggers it manually. Use this for on-demand workflows.
- **Scheduled** — The playbook runs automatically at a configured time. See [Running and Testing a Playbook](/graphium/playbooks/running-and-testing-a-playbook) for scheduling details.

You can also add an optional **Description** to the trigger to document when and why the playbook runs.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/graphium/playbooks/playbook-trigger-config.png" alt="Trigger configuration panel" />
</Frame>

---

### Step 6: Configure Resource Access

Click **Playbook Context** at the top of the Playbook Outline to open the resource access panel in the center column. This is where you grant the playbook access to the vault resources it needs.

The Playbook Context panel is organized into three sections:

**Tables** — Toggle on any vault tables the playbook needs to read from or write to. Each table shows its permission level (**Read** or **Read/Write**) and its approval mode (**Autonomous** or **Requires Approval**). Setting a table to **Requires Approval** causes the run to pause before any write action is taken on that table.

**Connections** — Toggle on any vault connections the playbook needs to use. Like tables, each connection has an approval mode. Set a connection to **Requires Approval** if you want the run to pause before sending data to an external system.

**Tools** — Toggle on any built-in tools the playbook may use during execution, such as Chart Generation or Web Search.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/graphium/playbooks/playbook-context-panel.png" alt="Playbook Context panel showing Tables, Connections, and Tools" />
</Frame>

> 💡 **Tip:** Start with the minimum access your workflow needs. You can always add more resources later by editing the playbook and publishing a new version.

---

### Step 7: Save as Draft

Click **Save** in the top-right header to save your current configuration as a draft version. The header shows the current version number and draft status (for example, *v1 · Draft*).

Drafts are not active — saving does not run or publish the playbook. You can save multiple times as you refine the playbook. Each save creates a new version snapshot you can return to via **History**.

---

### Step 8: Publish the Playbook

When you are ready to make the playbook active, click **Publish** in the top-right header. This promotes your current draft to the active published version.

Once published:

- The playbook shows an **Enabled** badge in the list and in the editor header.
- Manual playbooks are immediately available to run.
- Scheduled playbooks begin running at the next configured time.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/graphium/playbooks/playbook-enabled-badge.png" alt="Playbook list showing Enabled badge" />
</Frame>

> **Note:** Only Builders and Admins can publish a playbook. At least one step with instructions must be configured before publishing.

---

## Version History

Every save creates an immutable version snapshot. Click **History** in the top-right header to view the version history. You can re-publish any earlier version to roll back to a previous configuration.

---

## Editing a Published Playbook

To edit an active playbook, open it from the list and make your changes in the editor. Changes are saved as a new draft and do not affect the currently active published version until you publish again.

---

## Disabling or Deleting a Playbook

To stop a playbook from running without deleting it, disable it. A disabled playbook will not fire on schedule and cannot be triggered manually until re-enabled.

To permanently remove a playbook, delete it from the playbook list. Deletion cannot be undone.

---

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Likely Cause | What to Do |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| Playbooks does not appear in the sidebar | Your vault role is Contributor or Viewer | Ask your vault Admin or Builder to adjust your role |
| Publish button is not available | No steps have been configured, or no instructions have been added | Add at least one step with instructions before publishing |
| A table or connection is not available in Playbook Context | The resource has not been added to this vault | Navigate to the Tables or Connections section and add it first |
