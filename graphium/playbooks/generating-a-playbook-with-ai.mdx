## Overview

When you create a new playbook, Graphium AI asks you to describe what you want it to do. The AI reads your description and generates a complete draft: a playbook name, a suggested trigger, and a set of ordered steps with written instructions — all ready to open in the editor and refine.

This means you can go from idea to a working first draft in seconds, without having to build the step structure from scratch.

---

## Opening the Create Playbook Modal

1. In your vault sidebar, click **Playbooks**.
2. Click **+ New Playbook**.
3. The **Create Playbook** modal opens.

## Filling Out the Modal

The modal has two fields:

**AI Assistant**

A dropdown that controls which assistant this playbook is associated with. Selecting a specific assistant links the playbook to that assistant's context — the AI will be aware of that assistant's configured tables and tools when generating steps. Leave it set to **General** if the playbook is not tied to a specific assistant.

**Describe the playbook**

A free-text field (up to 5,000 characters) where you describe what you want the playbook to do. Write in plain language — the more specific you are, the better the output.

Once you've filled in your description, click **Generate Playbook**.

  <img src="/images/graphium/playbooks/create-playbook-modal.png" alt="Create Playbook modal with AI Assistant dropdown and description field" />

## What the AI Generates

After a few seconds, the editor opens with a fully drafted playbook. The AI produces:

- **A playbook name** derived from your description
- **A trigger type** inferred from your description — for example, a description mentioning "every week" or "every morning" will produce a Scheduled trigger
- **An ordered list of steps**, each with a title and written instructions explaining what the AI should do at that step

The playbook opens in the editor as **v1 · Draft**, ready for you to review and refine.

## What Still Needs to Be Configured

The AI writes the step instructions but does not automatically wire up the data context. After generation, each step will show **"No tables, connections, or tools selected yet."** until you connect the tables, connections, and tools the step needs.

For each step:

1. Click the step in the **Playbook Outline**.
2. Under **Context**, click **+ Add Context**.
3. Add the tables, connections, or tools that step should have access to.

You should also review the generated instructions in each step and edit any details the AI may have assumed — field names, filter conditions, or specific values that should match your vault's actual data.

When you're done refining, click **Save**, then **Publish** to make the playbook active.

> **Note:** See [Creating and Publishing a Playbook](/graphium/playbooks/creating-and-publishing-a-playbook) for a full walkthrough of configuring steps, context, and publishing.

## Tips for Writing Better Descriptions

The quality of the generated steps depends heavily on how clearly you describe the goal. A few tips:

- **Include a time or trigger signal** if you want a scheduled playbook — phrases like "every Monday", "daily at 9am", or "each week" will produce a Scheduled trigger.
- **Name the data you're working with** — mentioning table names like "Patients" or "Providers" helps the AI reference the right data in its step instructions.
- **Describe the outcome, not just the process** — for example, "send each patient a reminder 7 days before their appointment" is more useful than "check appointments and send messages".
- **Include any conditions** — "only for patients with a status of Active" or "if no provider is assigned" will be reflected in the generated step instructions.
