## Overview

Graphium AI surfaces in-app notifications when important events occur in your workspace or vault — connection failures, playbook issues, and membership changes. Notifications appear in the bell icon in the global navigation bar and are personal to each user; what you read or dismiss does not affect anyone else's notification feed.

---

## What Triggers a Notification

Notifications are generated by four categories of events:

**Connection health changes**

When a connection's health status changes to UNHEALTHY, ERROR, or DEAD, Graphium sends a notification to alert vault Admins and Builders. This covers any automated connection type — Graphium EMR, SFTP, Google Sheets, and others. A healthy connection that degrades without warning is the most common reason to check your notification feed.

**Playbook run failures and approval requests**

When a playbook run changes to a FAILED state or reaches an AWAITING_APPROVAL step (a review checkpoint requiring human sign-off before continuing), a notification is sent to vault Admins and Builders. This ensures the right people are alerted when automation stops or needs intervention, without having to monitor the Run History panel manually.

**Workspace member role and status changes**

When a workspace member's role or account status changes — for example, if someone is promoted, demoted, or deactivated — the affected member receives a notification, as do workspace Owners and Admins.

**Vault member role and status changes**

When a vault member's role changes — for example, if a user is added to a vault as a Builder or has their role changed — the affected member is notified, along with vault Admins.

---

## Who Receives Each Notification

Notifications are scoped by the context of the event:

- **Connection health** and **playbook run** notifications are delivered to Admins and Builders within the vault where the connection or playbook lives.
- **Workspace membership** notifications are delivered to workspace Owners and Admins, as well as the affected member.
- **Vault membership** notifications are delivered to vault Admins and the affected member.

If you are not an Admin or Builder in a vault, you will not receive notifications for events in that vault.

## Viewing Your Notifications

The bell icon appears in the top navigation bar, visible from anywhere in the application. When you have unread notifications in the current workspace, a badge on the icon displays the unread count. The count reflects only unread notifications — notifications you have already read are not included in the badge, even if you have not dismissed them.

Click the bell icon to open the notification dropdown. The dropdown displays your **active notifications** — all notifications that are either unread or read but not yet dismissed. Dismissed notifications do not appear.

Each notification shows what event occurred, which vault or workspace it relates to, and when it was generated. Unread notifications are visually distinguished from read ones. If there are no active notifications, the dropdown shows an empty state indicating your notification feed is clear.

---

## Acting on Notifications

**Mark one as read**

Click the read action on an individual notification to mark it as read. This removes the unread indicator and reduces the badge count, but the notification remains in your dropdown as an active notification until you dismiss it.

**Mark all as read**

Use the "Mark all as read" option to mark every unread notification in the dropdown as read at once. This clears the badge count without removing any notifications from your feed.

**Dismiss one notification**

Click the dismiss action on an individual notification to remove it from your active feed. Dismissed notifications no longer appear in the dropdown.

**Dismiss all notifications**

Use the "Dismiss all" option to clear your entire active notification feed at once. All notifications in the current workspace — read and unread — are dismissed from your view.

> **Note:** Your notification state is personal. Marking a notification as read or dismissing it only affects what you see. Other users who received the same notification will still see it as unread in their own feed until they act on it.

---

## Navigating from a Notification

Some notifications include a direct link to the relevant page in Graphium. Clicking the notification body (not the dismiss control) will navigate you there:

- **Connection health notifications** navigate to the Connections page for the affected vault.
- **Playbook run notifications** navigate to the Playbooks page for the affected vault.
- **Membership notifications** (workspace or vault role and status changes) do not include a navigation route in the current version.

---

## Delivery and Notification State

**In-app delivery only** — Notifications appear in the bell dropdown within the Graphium AI web application. There is no email, Slack, or push notification delivery in the current version. Notification creation is also non-blocking — generating a notification never delays or prevents the event that triggered it from completing.

**Personal notification state** — Every notification is tracked independently per user. When Graphium generates a notification for an event — for example, a connection failure in a vault with three Admins — each Admin receives their own separate copy with their own read and dismissed state. Reading or dismissing a notification in your feed has no effect on any other user's feed.
