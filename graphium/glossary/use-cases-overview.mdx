## Overview

Graphium AI is designed to work across industries and data environments, but its roots are in healthcare operations -- specifically clinical practice management. This section outlines the primary use cases the platform supports today and provides guidance for building your own.

---

## Clinical Practice Management

Healthcare groups face a unique operational challenge: high-volume clinical data generated across multiple surgical facilities, all of which must be captured accurately, reported on for compliance, and optimized for revenue. Graphium addresses three core workflows in this domain.

### Surgical Facility Case Management

The charge capture and billing pipeline -- from case documentation to submitted claims. Graphium connects to facility EMRs, captures case data in real time, validates records with AI, and tracks every case through the billing lifecycle (Submitted, In Review, Ready to Bill, Paid). AI assistants analyze turnover times, flag data entry errors, and catch missing signatures before they become claim denials.

**Key connections**: Epic (HL7), Graphium EMR, FTP/SFTP file servers, OR vitals monitors, Google Sheets

### Quality & Compliance Monitoring

MACRA/MIPS compliance management across a multi-site group. Graphium normalizes case data, quality measures, patient satisfaction surveys, and compliance documentation into a single vault. AI assistants continuously monitor provider performance against benchmarks, distinguish between clinical and documentation issues, and create prioritized tasks when intervention is needed.

**Key connections**: Graphium EMR, Graphium Surveys, Google Sheets

### Provider Scheduling & Workforce Management

Multi-site provider scheduling with coverage gap prediction and self-service request management. Graphium structures the scheduling puzzle -- providers, facilities, credentials, preferences, and requests -- into connected tables. AI assistants analyze coverage patterns, predict gaps before they become day-of emergencies, and let providers submit time-off requests and shift swaps through natural language.

**Key connections**: Google Sheets, Graphium EMR, Microsoft Teams, Twilio SMS

## SaaS Operations

Graphium's vault-and-AI model applies beyond healthcare. For SaaS companies, the same pattern -- fragmented data across tools, passive dashboards, and disconnected workflows -- creates the same opportunity for AI-driven intelligence.

### Financial Intelligence & Revenue Operations

Company financials unified from QuickBooks, Salesforce, product databases, and spreadsheets. AI assistants track SaaS metrics (MRR, ARR, burn rate, runway, net revenue retention), generate board-ready reports on demand, and proactively alert on churn signals by cross-referencing subscription data against usage patterns.

**Key connections**: QuickBooks Online, Google Sheets, PostgreSQL, Slack

### Customer Success & Account Intelligence

Customer lifecycle management from onboarding through renewal. Graphium connects CRM data, product usage, support tickets, NPS surveys, and team communication into a single account intelligence vault. AI assistants monitor health scores, predict churn from converging signals, track onboarding milestones, and automate the CS playbook with workflows for at-risk alerts, detractor follow-ups, and weekly summaries.

**Key connections**: HubSpot, PostgreSQL, Graphium EMR, Graphium Surveys, Slack

## Building Your Own Use Case

Graphium is not limited to the scenarios above. Any workflow that involves data from multiple sources, requires ongoing monitoring, and benefits from AI analysis can be modeled as a Graphium vault.

### Step 1: Identify Your Data Sources

List the systems where your data lives today. EMRs, spreadsheets, databases, CRMs, survey tools, file servers -- each becomes a potential connection. The more sources you connect, the more cross-referencing intelligence the AI can provide.

### Step 2: Design Your Vault Structure

Decide what tables you need. Think about your primary data (cases, customers, transactions) and your reference data (providers, facilities, products, categories). Use Groups to nest related fields. Use References to link tables together rather than duplicating data.

### Step 3: Connect Your Sources

Set up connections to your external systems. Start with your primary data source -- the system that generates the most records. Add supplementary sources (surveys, spreadsheets, reference databases) as additional dimensions of intelligence.

### Step 4: Configure Your AI Assistants

Create assistants scoped to specific roles. A financial analyst doesn't need access to HR data. A quality monitor doesn't need to see scheduling. Use table access controls (None / Read / Read+Write) to give each assistant exactly the data it needs. Write system prompts that define the assistant's expertise, priorities, and tone.

### Step 5: Set Up Workflows and Automation

Start with the workflows that replace manual monitoring: daily scans, weekly summaries, event-triggered alerts. Add autonomous actions to your assistants for the intelligence that should run without prompting. Let the system find issues before your team has to look for them.

### Step 6: Measure Impact

Track the shift: how much time was spent assembling reports before versus now? How many issues were caught proactively that would have been discovered too late? How has the team's focus moved from data assembly to decision-making?
